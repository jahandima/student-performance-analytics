**Step 7 — Effect Size Using Cohen's d**

Your Step 7 finding was unexpected — Overachievers had MORE absences than Underachievers. Cohen's d will tell you if that difference is meaningful or just noise.

Hints:
- Cohen's d formula: `d = (mean1 - mean2) / pooled_std`
- Pooled std = `sqrt((std1² + std2²) / 2)`
- Calculate d for `absences` between Underachievers and Overachievers
- Calculate d for `failures` between the same two groups
- Interpret your result:

| Cohen's d | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 0.0 – 0.2 | Negligible |
| 0.2 – 0.5 | Small |
| 0.5 – 0.8 | Medium |
| 0.8+ | Large |
